üìà The 4-Pillar Strategy

The Oracle evaluates every trade against four mandatory pillars of confluence. If all four don't align, the "Bouncer" will reject the trade.

Pillar 1: Weekly Bias (The Gatekeeper)

We do not look at Monday's open. We look at the Previous Week's Candle Structure.

  • Bullish Bias: Previous weekly close is above the open.
  • Bearish Bias: Previous weekly close is below the open.
  • The Top Wick Rule: If the previous week's top wick is >2% of the price, LONGS ARE BLOCKED. This indicates institutional selling pressure.

Pillar 2: VPVR Brick Walls

Using 180 days of volume data, the terminal identifies "Brick Walls" (High Volume Nodes).

  • Support Walls: HEAVY volume nodes below current price.
  • Resistance Walls: HEAVY volume nodes above current price.
  • We only enter trades that have "room to run" between these walls.

Pillar 3: CME Gaps

Bitcoin price is a magnet for unfilled CME gaps.

  • The terminal calculates the "Magnet Effect" based on proximity to these gaps.
  • You can manually set verified gaps using setgap to ensure the highest accuracy.

Pillar 4: Confirmation Signals (RSI & MACD)

  • RSI (14): Wilder's Smoothed RSI to detect overbought/oversold conditions.
  • MACD (12, 26, 9): Momentum histogram + signal line crosses.
  • Divergence Engine: Automatically detects Bullish/Bearish divergences where price and momentum disagree.
